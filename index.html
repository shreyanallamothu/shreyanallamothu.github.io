I passed the nation's first law protecting child influencers.

At age 13, I accidentally scrolled into tech policy. While watching YouTube, I discovered child influencers, which are children with large online followings who are often exploited by their parents for views. I saw many kids forced into videos, filmed without consent, and stripped of privacy. When I realized there were no laws protecting them, I took into action.

I wrote a legal memo outlining my legislation, and I reached out to (read: cold-called) every member of the Illinois Senate to find a sponsor. Finally, in 2023, I got the attention of Illinois State Senator David Koehler. We filed SB1782, the nation’s first law protecting kidfluencers. In March 2023, I testified for the bill in the Senate Labor Committee. The bill passed out of committee unanimously, and became Illinois law in August 2023.

Since then, I’ve worked with engineers at Meta and Google to develop screening tools that help minors request removal of exploitative online content. My advocacy has been featured by The New York Times, CNN, and The Washington Post, and I’ve passed similar legislation in 8 states, including California and Utah.
